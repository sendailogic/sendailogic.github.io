Sendai Logic School 2016 at Tohoku University is a winter school on computability theory and related fields. Several tutorial lectures will be given for graduate students and novice researchers. Admission is free, but please book by e-mail.
If you have any questions, please free to ask us at "sendailogicschool[at]gmail.com".

14:45-15:45 Yang Yue
Seetapun's theorem revisited [Slide]
[Abstract] In the study of Ramsey's Theorem in reverse mathematics, Seetapun's result(s) in 1995 played an important role. He developed a new technique for cone avoiding and showed that Ramsey's Theorem for pairs does not imply ACA0. In this talk, I will present a proof of his theorem. Based on some ideas obtained from later studies, this proof is more friendly to graduate students.

How to prove it: Phase transitions in logic [Slide]
[Abstract] Using the example of Dickson's lemma, we show how to prove phase transition results in independence, including the sharpening results. This talk will be suitable for graduate students.
